Defend Dad equips fathers with practical tools, plain-language legal guidance, and community support to secure fair parenting time, child-support outcomes, and due process.

Custody by the Numbers

~80% Mothers are custodial parents (2018)
~20% Fathers are custodial parents (2018)
~65% Average time awarded to mothers
~35% Average time awarded to fathers

Nationally, dads receive ~54% as much parenting time as mothers.

Child Support Reality

$400–$500 Typical monthly child support (national averages)
50/50 Still often requires payments if income differs

Even in equal-time cases, many states order support based on income formulas—often requiring the higher earner to pay.

Our 50/50 Stance

50/50 should be the default unless clear, proven cause shows otherwise. In true equal-time arrangements, no parent should pay child support to the other. Each parent fully supports their child during their own parenting time—covering housing, food, clothing, and activities—without subsidizing an ex-partner’s household.
